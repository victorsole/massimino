## 2024 fitness hashtag trends shaping both platforms

The fitness hashtag landscape saw significant shifts in 2024, driven by emerging workout modalities and platform algorithm changes.

**Pilates experienced explosive growth** with a **105% year-over-year increase** in TikTok posts. The Wall Pilates challenge alone generated **42.6 million views**, and #pilates now averages over 10,000 views per TikTok video despite having fewer total videos than traditional gym content. This reflects broader wellness culture penetration into mainstream fitness.

**Strength training content surged 38%** across platforms in 2024, with #strengthtraining showing consistent growth as functional fitness and resistance training gained cultural cachet. The "strong not skinny" movement accelerated this trend, particularly among female creators.

The **#ShyGirlWorkout** phenomenon reached **200+ million views**, revealing demand for accessible, anxiety-friendly gym content. This trend spawned related hashtags like #GymAnxiety and #FirstTimeGym that perform well for beginner-focused creators.

**Platform-native community tags** now rival traditional hashtags: #GymTok (343.2B views), #FitTok (61.1B views), and #fitnesstiktok create algorithm-friendly discovery pathways unavailable on Instagram.

## Engagement benchmarks and strategic insights

Understanding engagement rates helps contextualize raw hashtag numbers for content strategy decisions.

**Instagram fitness benchmarks (2025)**: The average fitness brand sees **0.55% engagement rate** with posts using hashtags receiving approximately **12.6% more engagement** than posts without. Fitness micro-influencers achieve notably higher rates around **3.85%**. Research from Later analyzing 18 million posts found that **20 hashtags yields the highest average reach**, despite Instagram's official recommendation of 3-5.

**TikTok fitness benchmarks**: Fitness content averages **9.3% engagement**—dramatically higher than Instagram. TikTok fitness influencers have the **highest engagement rates of any platform** according to Future Fit research. However, TikTok's algorithm weights content quality over hashtags, making the optimal range **3-7 hashtags**.

**Growth context**: The global fitness market reached **$223.19 billion in 2024 revenue**, with online fitness projected to grow at **33.1% CAGR** through 2027. This economic backdrop drives continued hashtag volume growth, particularly in digital-native categories like home workouts and app-based training.

## Data methodology and limitations

Statistics were compiled from TikTokHashtags.com (May 2024 data), eDigital Agency (2025 updates), Inflact real-time analytics, Statista, and marketing platforms including Hootsuite, Later, and Sprout Social. 

**Important caveats**: TikTok view counts are cumulative and may have increased **20-40%** since May 2024 collection dates. Instagram post counts represent lifetime totals since 2010, not current active usage. Some niche hashtags (#chestday, #gains, #cardio) lack precise public metrics—ranges represent estimates from marketing platform research. Different sources occasionally report varying figures due to collection timing; where conflicts existed, more recent and corroborated data was prioritized.

## Conclusion

The fitness hashtag ecosystem in 2024/2025 reveals platform-specific opportunities rather than universal best practices. **TikTok rewards entertainment and authenticity**—#gymhumor's 30,000+ average views dwarfs instructional content—while **Instagram remains the repository of record** for fitness brands seeking searchable, evergreen visibility. 

For European markets, regional hashtags like **#fitfrenchies** (1.7M posts) and **#fitfamgermany** offer substantially better engagement potential than competing in the 563-million-post #fitness ocean. The **105% pilates growth** and emergence of accessibility-focused hashtags like #ShyGirlWorkout signal content whitespace opportunities for 2025.

Strategic hashtag deployment now requires platform-specific approaches: maximize Instagram with 20 mixed-competition hashtags, prioritize TikTok's platform-native community tags (#GymTok, #FitTok) over traditional taxonomy, and consider regional language hashtags for European audience building.
