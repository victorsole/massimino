# Victor Hypertrophy & Fat Loss Workout Routine

**Duration:** 12 Weeks (March – May 2026)  
**Reassessment Date:** Beginning of June 2026  
**Designed for:** Victor, 38 years old, 22+ years of training experience  
**Frequency:** 4 sessions/week (flexible to 3 or 5)  
**Session Length:** 60 minutes maximum  
**Supplements:** Creatine (daily), ESN Whey Isolate (morning, post-workout, optional pre-bed + magnesium)

---

## Program Philosophy

This program is built on the evidence from Van Every et al. (2026) and the Massimino knowledge base. The core principles:

- **Mechanical tension is the driver.** Every session prioritizes compound movements with progressive overload.
- **No time wasted chasing hormonal spikes or "the pump."** Every minute of your 60 is purposeful.
- **Progressive overload across the 12 weeks.** Volume and intensity increase in structured phases.
- **Fat loss comes from the energy deficit created by training + daily activity + reasonable nutrition.** No extreme diets needed.
- **The step machine stays.** Your 15–20 min warmup doubles as steady-state cardio for caloric expenditure and cardiovascular health. Smart use of limited time.

---

## Program Structure

The 12 weeks are divided into three 4-week mesocycles:

| Phase | Weeks | Focus | Rep Ranges | RIR Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1: Foundation** | 1–4 | Re-establish progressive overload, moderate volume | 8–12 reps | 2–3 RIR |
| **Phase 2: Accumulation** | 5–8 | Increase volume, introduce intensity techniques | 6–12 reps | 1–2 RIR |
| **Phase 3: Intensification** | 9–12 | Peak intensity, slight volume reduction, push limits | 5–10 reps | 0–1 RIR |

**Deload:** Week 4 and Week 8, reduce volume by ~40% (drop 1–2 sets per exercise, use same weight). This isn't laziness — it's how experienced lifters manage fatigue accumulation across 22+ years of training.

## Progressive Overload Protocol

**The single most important principle in this program.** Every week, try to do at least one of the following on each exercise:

1. **Add weight** (even 1–2 kg counts).
2. **Add a rep** within the prescribed range.
3. **Add a set** (within the phase prescription).

**Tracking:** Log every session. Weight, reps, sets. If you hit the top of the rep range for all prescribed sets, increase the load next session by the smallest increment available.

## Nutrition Guidelines (Victor-Style)

You don't need a strict diet. You need a few strategic habits:

**Protein target:** Aim for ~1.6–2.2 g/kg bodyweight daily. Your ESN Whey Isolate shakes (morning + post-workout + occasional pre-bed) will cover ~60–90 g of that. The rest comes from whole foods.

**Creatine:** Continue your daily 3–5 g. Timing doesn't matter much — consistency does.

**For fat loss without misery:**

- Eat your protein first at every meal.
- Don't restrict food groups — just be mindful of portions on calorie-dense foods.
- Your pasta is fine. Just pair it with a solid protein source and vegetables.
- Keep alcohol moderate (it blunts MPS and adds empty calories).
- Stay hydrated — especially with creatine supplementation.

**The magnesium before bed is a good call.** It supports sleep quality and recovery, which are underrated for both hypertrophy and fat loss.

- At 38 with 22+ years of training, you're past the rapid-gains phase. Every kilogram of muscle is earned. Respect the process.

## Final Notes

- **Consistency > Perfection.** Four solid sessions per week for 12 weeks beats six sessions for 3 weeks then burnout.
- **Your family and Beresol come first.** This program is designed to fit your life, not the other way around.

- **At almost 39, recovery is your competitive advantage.** Sleep well. Manage stress. Take the deload weeks seriously.
